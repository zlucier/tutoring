My teaching philosophy

I am a firm believer that anyone can be great at math. Many of the struggles people experience with the subject come from forgotten knowledge from previous years since the nature of math is cumulative and sequential at the high school level. It is my mission to close these gaps and to make definitions, results, and methods easier to remember by motivating them. I also want to make the whole subject of math more approachable by explaining why mathematical abstraction is useful in the context of solving seemingly unrelated problems with the same method.
